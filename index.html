Overview

Bringing intelligence into the field.

Most agricultural automation still assumes rigid worlds. Plants bend, fruit detaches unpredictably, and every canopy is different. We are building AI that understands physical interaction with living crops — and transfers that knowledge from simulation to real robots.

Our Thesis

Physical AI for agriculture should be crop-agnostic.

A brain that understands plants should not be limited by robot or task type.

We tackle plant manipulation in its full generality.

One unified policy stack — assess, harvest, prune — across any crop.

Platforms

Robot applications that deliver real-world value.

To stay grounded on our journey, we build capabilities that work in actual orchards, greenhouses, and packing lines.

Perception & Assessment

Vision models interpret plant geometry beneath dense canopy — detecting fruit maturity, stem compliance, and regions that must not be damaged.

Harvest & Manipulation

Contact-rich control policies for harvesting, pruning, pick-and-place, and selective cutting — learned in simulation, deployed on real arms.

Simulation Pipeline

Procedural orchards from Helios, imported into Isaac Sim for camera data, physics interaction, and RL training — one command, any scale.

Training

Trained in simulation

Thousands of randomized plant morphologies, lighting conditions, and contact scenarios — generated in parallel. Training cycles that would take years in the field happen overnight.

Deployment

Deployed on real hardware

The same policy weights run directly on field robots. Sim-to-real transfer is built into the training objective from day one — not bolted on later.
