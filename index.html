The Problem

Plants are not rigid. Traditional robots assume they are.

Every stem bends differently. Every fruit detaches differently. Every contact is uncertain.

Conventional automation breaks when the environment changes — new cultivars, dense canopy, variable lighting, irregular growth, or unpredictable contact forces.

Agricultural robotics does not need another scripted demo. It needs intelligence that generalizes physical interaction.

What It Does

One brain. Multiple capabilities. No brittle scripts.

Understands plant structure

Vision and segmentation models interpret plant geometry beneath dense canopy:

  • Identify regions that must not be damaged

Learns physical interaction
